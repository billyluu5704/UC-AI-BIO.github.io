Nate Steffen is a PhD candidate in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati.
He previously earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering with
certificates in Mathematics and Computer Science from UC.

During his undergraduate studies, Nate completed multiple co-op and research rotations that
shaped his interest in AI and space robotics. His early work included two semesters as a
research assistant in mechanical engineering, where he conducted molecular dynamics
simulations. He then completed a co-op semester with the Air Force Research Laboratory,
focusing on machine learning applications. Following this, Nate undertook two co-op rotations
at NASA Glenn Research Center as a **Pathways Intern**, developing optimization code and
supporting the design of cislunar low-thrust spacecraft trajectories. He continues to work at
NASA Glenn during academic breaks.

Nate’s research centers on the development and assurance of **responsible AI** for space
robotics. As part of the **Strategic Space Technologies Initiative (SSTI)**, he works on the
verification and validation of AI techniques used in space proximity operations. Much of his
research involves optimization of robotic arms for on-orbit servicing and inspection
procedures, and he is also exploring the use of AI for path planning and autonomy in other
key space robotics missions.
