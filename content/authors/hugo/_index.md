Hugo Henry is a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati, working
under the guidance of Dr. Kelly Cohen at the AI Bio Lab. His research focuses on control and
command of aerospace systems using **fuzzy logic**, with particular emphasis on airspace safety,
air mobility, and the development of explainable and certifiable AI for aviation.

He previously completed a Master of Engineering in Aerospace Engineering through the AESOP
exchange program at UC, as well as both a Master IMSAT (Avionics major) and a Bachelor IMSAT
(Embedded Systems major) at the University of Bordeaux in France.

### Relevant Experience
- **2022–2024 – Apprenticeship/Internship**, Quality Analyst  
  *Sopra Steria Aeroline*, Mérignac, France

- **2022 – Internship**, Control and Command  
  *Safran Helicopter Engines*, Bordes, France

### Current Project
**Fuzzy Rule–Based Aircraft Anti-Collision System**  
Hugo is developing a fuzzy-logic-based system to predict hazardous zones and generate evasive
commands to prevent potential incidents. This work also aims to demonstrate that AI-driven
control systems can achieve a level of explainability sufficient for certification by aviation
authorities such as the **FAA** and **EASA**.
