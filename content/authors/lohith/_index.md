Lohith works on advancing explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) for cybersecurity, 
aiming to build machine learning models that are both effective and accountable. His 
research focuses on transforming opaque ML systems into auditable, analyst-ready tools 
capable of surfacing threats early while providing faithful, attack-resistant explanations. 
These explanations support incident response and regulatory compliance, ensuring that 
AI decisions can be trusted and verified rather than treated as black-box alerts.

Additionally, Lohith works on autonomous AI decision-making for adversarial and 
uncertain environments. His work emphasizes the development of verifiable, human-aware 
AI agents that integrate uncertainty estimation, causal reasoning, and safety guardrails. 
These mechanisms ensure that autonomous systems can operate independently under 
adversarial conditions while still deferring control to humans when appropriate.

His overarching goal is to build AI systems that detect and respond effectively to security 
threats while also justifying their reasoning — earning human trust in real-world 
deployments.
