Bharadwaj “Ben” Dogga is a PhD candidate in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati.
His research centers on advancing **explainable machine learning** to support decision making in
complex engineered systems. Ben develops predictive frameworks for component life estimation,
sensor optimization, and anomaly detection, enabling improved system reliability, resilience, and
operational efficiency.

His work spans interpretable models, dimensionality reduction methods, and hybrid machine
learning pipelines tailored for real-world, safety-critical applications. Ben is also involved in
projects related to **time series anomaly detection** for Activities of Daily Living and
**predictive health monitoring** for jet engine components.

He frequently shares his research through conference presentations and collaborations, with the
aim of strengthening the link between AI-based technical decision making and practical
engineering deployment.
