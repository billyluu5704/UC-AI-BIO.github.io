Jared is an Aerospace Engineering PhD student at the AI Bio Lab and a Digital Futures
Fellow. He earned his B.S. in Aerospace Engineering & Engineering Mechanics from the
University of Cincinnati in 2021. Previously, he worked as a Machine Learning intern at
THALES Avionics, where he explored new applications of Evolutionary Fuzzy Systems (EFS)
and contributed to expanding their core methodological capabilities.

His current research focuses on the verification of machine-learning-based and classical
control systems, with a specialization in Fuzzy Logic Control (FLC). His work spans three
verification paradigms:

**Formal Methods** — providing mathematical guarantees of requirement satisfaction  
**Probabilistic Methods** — offering statistical evidence of safety and robustness  
**Interventionist Methods** — runtime strategies that anticipate and mitigate requirement violations  

Promising techniques within these paradigms include reachability analysis, stochastic
robustness analysis, and runtime assurance.

Currently, he is applying reachability analysis tools to assess the safety of satellite
servicing and inspection missions and is developing new tools to enable formal analysis
of fuzzy logic controllers.
