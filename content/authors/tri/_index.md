Tri Nguyen is a Doctor of Philosophy student in Aerospace Engineering and Engineering Mechanics
at the University of Cincinnati, with an anticipated graduation in 2028. He previously completed
his Bachelor of Science in Aerospace Engineering at UC (2020–2024), earning a GPA of 3.99/4.0.

At the AI Bio Lab, Tri’s research spans multiple interdisciplinary projects that connect
aerospace engineering with advanced AI methods. He has contributed to the development of AI
models for predicting psychiatric consultation outcomes and detecting LLM jailbreak attempts in
collaboration with UC’s College of Medicine. His aerospace-related work includes the design of
**IntroSat**, a self-balancing CubeSat simulator for attitude control research, and the
optimization of an indoor positioning system for autonomous navigation studies.

Tri’s professional experience includes serving as a **Teaching Assistant for Flight Mechanics**,
a **Manufacturing Engineer Intern at Bosch**, and a **Production & Operations Management Intern
at HH Global**. Across his academic career, he has earned numerous prestigious awards and
fellowships—including the **UGR Fellowship**, **UPRISE Fellowship**, **Co-op/Research of the
Year Award**, **Tau Beta Pi Scholarship**, and the **SRIDE Undergraduate Fellowship**—recognizing
his excellence in research, innovation, and technical leadership.
