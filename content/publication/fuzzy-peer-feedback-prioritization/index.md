abstract: |
    Student peer review systems generate substantial feedback, but the volume often overwhelms students, making it difficult to identify actionable insights. This paper presents a fuzzy logic-based approach to intelligently prioritize peer feedback by considering frequency, sentiment, and detail. We compare three methods: raw unprocessed feedback, simple heuristic averaging, and fuzzy logic evaluation. Results from 30 students with 150 peer reviews demonstrate that fuzzy logic provides more selective prioritization (r = 0.901 with simple methods) while meaningfully integrating feedback detail (r = 0.300, p < 0.001), 
    a dimension showing no significant correlation in simple averaging (r = -0.123, p = 0.135). The fuzzy system reduces HIGH priority classifications by 15.4% while maintaining critical feedback elevation, resulting in more actionable guidance for students. Our approach demonstrates the value of fuzzy logic for handling uncertainty in educational contexts where explainability is paramount.
