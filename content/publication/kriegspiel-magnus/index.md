abstract: |
    Kriegspiel, the imperfect-information variant of chess, poses a challenging testbed for decision-making under hidden state, sparse feedback, and fog of war. Existing approaches, including metaposition-based search and Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), achieve competent defensive play but remain largely reactive and tightly coupled to the specific structure of Kriegspiel [1, 2].
    We propose a hybrid fuzzy–neural architecture that learns relational spatial structure, patterns of coordination, threat, and control. Evaluations against established baselines indicate that the model achieves strong positional outcomes despite limitations in endgame resolution. This work takes a step toward imperfect-information game agents that reason strategically rather than relying solely on probabilistic rollout or heuristic search.
