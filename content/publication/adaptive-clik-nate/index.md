abstract: |
    As humanity’s presence in Earth’s orbit grows, so too does the amount of space debris. To ensure that there is proper room in Earth’s orbits to continue scientific missions and to allow for interplanetary travel, there needs to be a widespread method to manage debris autonomously. This work explores the use of Fuzzy Logic combined with the widely-used CLIK control to perform tumbling satellite capture safely and efficiently. By dynamically tuning the CLIK’s KP , KD, pi, and wjk gains using Genetic Fuzzy Trees, performance can be optimized without compromising safety. It was found that the Genetic Fuzzy System
    performs very well in its training case reducing both settling time and control effort compared to standard CLIK control without violating joint constraints or colliding, but it requires further training on more cases to be generalized better.
