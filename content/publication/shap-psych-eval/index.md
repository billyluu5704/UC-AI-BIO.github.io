abstract: Psychiatric consultation-liaison services often operate under resource constraints, making early and accurate prediction critical. This study analyzes electronic health records from 450 hospitalized patients to predict psychiatric consult needs using classification techniques. We applied feature engineering and SHAP-based selection to reduce 99 variables to 10 key features. Models trained on this refined set, including both clear-box and black-box classifiers, showed reduced overfitting and strong performance. Fuzzy Decision Tree offered the best balance of accuracy and interpretability. These results highlight the potential of explainable models to support timely psychiatric consultation and pave the way for future systems that also evaluate the necessity and impact of clinical benefit.
