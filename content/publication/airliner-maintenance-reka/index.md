abstract: |
    Aircraft advanced monitoring and predictive Maintenance are key objectives of the research aiming to streamline maintenance, improve efficiency, reduce turn around time (TAT) and cut costs. In order to follow the predicted increase of 44% in air traffic by 2050, the need to reduce this down time due to minor maintenance is increasing and we can see a silver lining through applied, trustworthy Artificial intelligence (AI). This paper focuses on minor fault detection in the position and movement of the moving parts of the body of an airliner digital twin. Through this paper we see how fuzzy rule-based systems (FRBS) can be used effectively within the realm of advanced monitoring and predictive maintenance. It also examines how the system could be implemented and
    it’s impact in the maintenance chains such as planning and organization of the maintenance. Other topics such as certification processes for AI systems and training will be briefly touched on. Hence, this paper is a concept presentation of an FRBS for anomaly detection and mild maintenance decision making to streamline non-critical degradations upon actuator systems. Through a dual simulation process comparing a nominal behavior under commands and an actual behavior coupled through a Fuzzy Inference System (FIS), we identify possible faults in order to redirect to the Part M personnel (airworthiness crew) to adapt the maintenance plans and mitigate wear and tear. This concept can be extended to any system equipped with sensors for monitoring and an established
    maintenance plan.
